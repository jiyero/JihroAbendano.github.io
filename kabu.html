KaBu

This is a thesis research conducted by thesis group members inculding myself which focuses on the development of an Artificial Eating Companion (AEC) named KaBu, designed to enhance the dining experience for solitary diners through advanced verbal and non-verbal communication capabilities. ​

The AEC’s responses are delivered through a text-to-speech module and speakers, ensuring a seamless and engaging user experience. The study addresses limitations in current AEC implementations, particularly the lack of naturalistic interaction due to constrained communication abilities. The AEC is physically embodied with a head, hands, and upper torso, enabling naturalistic interactions during meals. It communicates verbally using a Large Language Model (LLM) to engage in human-like conversations, while autonomously displaying facial exppressions and hand gestures to convey emotions.
